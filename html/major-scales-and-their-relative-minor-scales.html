Major scales and their relative minor scales

Major scales and their relative minor scales

Every major scale has a relative minor scale.

  • The relative minor of a major scale starts on the sixth note of the major scale.
  • The relative major of a minor scale starts on the third note of the minor scale.

Relative scales always share the same notes. We have already seen that do (C) major has la (A) minor as its relative. If we take the sol (G) major scale, its sixth note is mi (E), so its relative minor is (E) minor. When we build (mi) E minor following the pattern: T – ST – T – T – ST – T – T.

We can see that it has only one altered note: fa♯, just like sol (G) major. We know it is fa♯ and not sol ♭ because the note G natural is already used in the scale.
