Musical Language IV: scales

To sum up

To sum up

In Western music we use twelve different sounds, but they are not usually all used at the same time. Instead, they are selected to form scales. A scale is an ordered series of notes that follows a specific pattern of tones and semitones. The most important scales are the major and the minor scales, which have seven notes and a different characteristic sound. Each scale starts on a main note called the tonic and creates a tonality, which is the musical “atmosphere” of a piece. In addition, every major scale has a related minor scale that shares the same notes, called relative scales. There are other scales that you already know, such as the pentatonic scale, but for now we will focus on major and minor scales.
